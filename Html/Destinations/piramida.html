Pyramid of Tirana

The Pyramid of Tirana (Albanian: Piramida e Tiranës) is a structure and former museum located in Tirana, Albania.
It opened as a museum in 1988 and became a conference center in 1991 following the collapse of Communism.
During the 1999 Kosovo War, the building was used as a NATO base. In 2018, a new project was unveiled that would
turn the pyramid into a youth IT center for Creative Technologies with a focus on computer programming and robotics.

1988

On 14 October 1988, the structure opened as the Enver Hoxha Museum, originally serving as a museum about the legacy of Enver Hoxha, the long-time leader of Communist Albania, who had died three years earlier. The structure was co-designed by Hoxha's daughter Pranvera Hoxha, an architect, and her husband Klement Kolaneci, along with Pirro Vaso and Vladimir Bregu.[

1996

In 1996, the Pyramid of Tirana was still a prominent symbol of Albania’s communist past, though its function had already shifted from a museum to a cultural center. Despite its controversial design, it was a popular landmark, reflecting a mix of nostalgia and emerging post-communist identity in the city.

1992

After 1991, following the collapse of Communism, the Pyramid ceased its function as a museum and for several years was repurposed as a conference center and exhibition venue, as well as being rebranded with its current name.

1999

During the 1999 Kosovo War, the former museum was used as a base by NATO and humanitarian organizations.

2002

Since 2001, part of the Pyramid has been used as broadcasting center by Albanian media outlets Top Channel and Top Albania Radio, while the rest of the structure and the paved surrounding area (currently being used as a parking lot and bus station for minivans to Elbasan) have experienced dilapidation and vandalism.

2018

In 2018, a new project was unveiled that would turn the Pyramid into an IT center for youth called TUMO Tirana, with a focus on computer programming, robotics, and start ups. The project consists in building staircases on the sides of the pyramid, and glass coverage areas for increased natural light.

2019

In 2019, the Pyramid was also used as a filming location for part of a remake of the 1995 cult horror film Castle Freak.

2020

In 2020, the Pyramid of Tirana was undergoing significant reconstruction efforts to transform it into a modern cultural space. The project focused on preserving the iconic structure while adapting it to serve as a hub for exhibitions, events, and artistic initiatives, breathing new life into this once-controversial monument.

2023

The reconstruction of the Pyramid of Tirana was completed in 2023. The project aimed to revitalize the landmark and transform it into a modern cultural venue, while preserving its historical and architectural significance.

]

2025

In 2025, the Pyramid of Tirana has become a vibrant cultural hub, hosting exhibitions, events, and performances that celebrate both Albanian heritage and contemporary art. The modernized structure has been seamlessly integrated into the city's landscape, attracting both locals and tourists with its unique blend of history and innovation.
